# Lore — The Sunken Crown

Background on the world, the dungeon, and the trial. No spoilers. Safe to read before your first run.

---

## The Dungeon

The Sunken Crown predates Lord Malachar by centuries. Nobody alive knows who built it or why. The keep above it has changed hands many times — burned, rebuilt, inherited, sold. The dungeon beneath it has not changed at all.

It has always had something in it. Whatever that something is, it has never left.

---

## The Trial

Lord Malachar did not create the trial. He inherited a dungeon with a monster in it and recognised an opportunity. The rules are simple and have not changed since his grandfather's time.

You surrender everything you own before you enter. Malachar's men convert it to coin. The coin goes into the dungeon. If you walk out, you keep what you can carry. If you don't, Malachar keeps the entrance fee.

He has no particular feelings about which outcome occurs. Both are profitable. The monster is not his to control and he has never tried. He simply manages access and handles the paperwork.

The trial has been running long enough that nobody remembers a time before it. There are families in the town who have lost three generations to the dungeon. They still come. Desperation has a long memory and a short imagination.

---

## Lord Malachar

The last of a bloodline that has been tied to this place for two generations. He did not choose the dungeon — he was born above it. By the time he understood what was beneath the keep it was already too late to walk away, though he has never tried to explain why.

He is not cruel. He is not kind. He is a man who has made his peace with a business that runs on other people's last resorts, and that peace cost him something he cannot quite name.

He will be in the courtyard when you come out. If you come out.

---

## The Gold

Three hundred years of contestants have dropped their worldly goods down the chute into the dungeon. The coin accumulates. Whatever is at the heart of the dungeon has no use for it and makes no effort to protect it.

It simply sits there. A monument nobody intended to build, growing slowly in the dark.

You watched your own stake disappear down that chute before the gates closed. It is in there somewhere, indistinguishable from everyone else's last resort.

You are not going in for a prize. You are going in to take back what's yours — and as much else as you can carry.

---

## The Name

People began calling it the Sunken Crown long before Malachar's family arrived. The name predates the trial, predates the keep in its current form, predates most of what stands above ground.

Whatever is at the heart of the dungeon wears a crown. That much has always been known. Where the crown came from, and why it has never left, are questions the dungeon does not answer.
